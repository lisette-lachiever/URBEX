Key Insights

Three data-driven findings about how New York City moves

Insight 01
Rush Hour Kills Speed
During morning (7-9 AM) and evening (4-7 PM) commute windows, average taxi speeds are measurably lower than off-peak hours. Manhattan's grid becomes a bottleneck exactly when demand is highest a defining friction of dense urban life.
speed reduction vs off-peak
Insight 02
Weekends Mean Longer Trips
Weekend rides cover more distance on average than weekday trips. Without the pressure of office commutes, people travel further to outer boroughs, airports, and late-night venues that are harder to reach by subway.
extra distance on weekends
Insight 03
The City Flies at Night
A 2 AM trip moves at roughly double the speed of an 8 AM one. Empty streets let drivers cross the same distance in half the time. In NYC, taxi duration is determined by traffic, not by distance the clearest example of congestion pricing.
night speed vs morning rush
